About the Medical Department

The Medical Department at King Saud University is part of the College of Medicine, established in 1967. It was the first medical college in Saudi Arabia and has a rich history of providing high-quality medical education and training.

History

The College of Medicine was established in 1967 and has since grown to become a leading institution in medical education. The women's section was inaugurated in 1974 to advance female medical education. The college has graduated thousands of physicians and specialists over the years.

Vision

Our vision is to be a leading institution in medical education, fostering innovation, critical thinking, and excellence in the field of medicine.

Academic Programs

We offer a range of academic programs, including undergraduate and postgraduate degrees in various medical disciplines. Our programs are designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills needed for successful careers in medicine.

Faculty Members

Our faculty members are distinguished scholars and practitioners in the field of medicine, dedicated to providing high-quality education and mentorship to our students.

Research Areas

The Medical Department is actively involved in research across various areas of medicine, contributing to the development of medical knowledge and practice.
